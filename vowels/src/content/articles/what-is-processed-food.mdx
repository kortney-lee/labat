# What Is Processed Food, Really?

Most foods are processed somehow. The question is not whether processing exists. The question is whether processing strips food quality, adds high-risk ingredients, and drives overconsumption.

## The useful definition

A practical system is to look at ingredient complexity:

- minimally processed: one or few recognizable ingredients
- moderately processed: useful prep changes with limited additives
- ultra-processed: long ingredient lists with industrial additives and heavy flavor engineering

## Why this matters for households

The biggest risk pattern is not one single ingredient. It is repeated exposure to products designed for high palatability and low satiety.

## What to do this week

1. Compare two versions of one product category you buy often.
2. Prefer shorter ingredient lists when price and convenience are similar.
3. Keep one low-cost, minimally processed staple in every meal.
